Innovators, interested on solving problems using technology, bringing digital solutions to their communities and event creating their own start-up company, spring boarding Africa into the future

  • Become an innovator

    • Critical Thinking

      Maybe you think you have a great idea for a new product. Or, maybe you already created a product and your competitors just released an update that scares. Either way, you need a proven process for innovating and delivering fast. You need IBM® Design Thinking.

    • Ideation

      When you have a clear idea of the problem that you want to solve, you can employ one of several design-thinking techniques to envision solutions.

    • Personas

      Great design is anchored in a deep understanding of your user. After all, you are creating something for someone. That "someone" is a person, and the better you understand that person, the more engaging the design will be and the better it will solve the person's problem.

    • Wireframe Sketches

      When you work in software development, you inevitably will see a wireframe sketch of one kind or another. Wireframe sketches communicate the most basic aspects of the user interface and the most important task flows through your product. You can use wireframe sketches to quickly gather feedback from members of your target audience and learn more about what matters to them.

    • Watson Conversation

      Create chat bots and virtual agents that use natural language understanding and dialog to automate interactions with your users.

  • Work with a team

    • Building a culture and a team

      At its root, the IBM® Bluemix® Garage Method, including DevOps, is a cultural movement; it's all about people. An organization might adopt the most efficient processes or automated tools possible, but they're useless without the people who run the processes and use the tools. Therefore, building a culture is at the core of adopting the Bluemix Garage Method.

    • Organizational roles

      A common misconception about agile development is that it means everyone does everything. In fact, the opposite is true. To run a disciplined agile process, well-defined roles and responsibilities must exist.

    • Social Coding

      Social coding is a software development practice that emphasizes formal and informal collaboration. DevOps makes coding more social, and social coding makes better code.
